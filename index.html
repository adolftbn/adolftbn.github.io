About

Engineer in the loop

I'm a sixth-semester Electrical Engineering student from Bukittinggi, West Sumatra, now based in Purwokerto. My work lives at the intersection of embedded systems, IoT, and control theory — mostly on STM32 and ESP32, mostly chasing a stable setpoint.

I work as a lab assistant and stay active across research, competitions, and funded innovation projects. Most of what's below started as a class assignment and grew into something I kept building after the semester ended.

Featured Academic Projects

Three builds worth a closer look

Selected from nine semester projects — the ones with the most depth, the most hands, or a trophy attached.

CORDY — Coffee Renewable Drying System

Capstone Design · Semester 6 · Project Leader & System Architect

A PID-controlled coffee dryer that pairs closed-loop temperature control with real-time web monitoring, backed by solar power so drying doesn't stop when the grid does. I led the team and owned the system architecture end to end — sensing, control, power, and the dashboard tying it together.

ESP32PID ControlSolar BackupWeb MonitoringIoT

Autonomous Sumo Robot

Robotika · Semester 6 · Programmer & Hardware — Juara 1

An autonomous sumo-bot built around a finite-state machine for opponent detection and edge avoidance, tuned for aggressive-but-controlled arena behavior. Started as a class build; ended up winning 1st place once the instructor turned it into a competition.

ESP32FSMSensor FusionCompetition Winner

Smart Garden with Fuzzy Sugeno

Sistem IoT · Semester 5 · Embedded & IoT

An IoT irrigation system that reasons about watering the way a grower would — using a Sugeno fuzzy inference model instead of blunt if-else thresholds, monitored and controlled remotely through Blynk.
